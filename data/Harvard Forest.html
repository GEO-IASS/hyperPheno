Harvard Forest Data Archive

HF249

Radiometric and Meteorological Data from Harvard Forest Barn Tower Since 2011

Related Publications

Data

  • hf249-01: radiometric and meterological data (preview)

Overview

  • Lead: Andrew Richardson, Donald Aubrecht
  • Investigators:
  • Contact: Andrew Richardson
  • Start date: 2011-11-23
  • End date: 2017-06-14
  • Status: ongoing
  • Location: Prospect Hill Tract (Harvard Forest)
  • Latitude: +42.5353
  • Longitude: -72.1899
  • Elevation: 351 meter
  • Taxa: Acer rubrum (red maple), Pinus strobus (white pine), Quercus rubra (red oak)
  • Release date: 2015
  • Revisions: data updated 2017-08-31
  • EML file: knb-lter-hfr.249.2
  • DOI: digital object identifier
  • Related links:
    • Canopy-Atmosphere Exchange of Carbon, Water and Energy at Harvard Forest EMS Tower since 1991
    • Fisher Meteorological Station at Harvard Forest since 2001
    • PhenoCam Images and Canopy Phenology at Harvard Forest since 2008
  • Study type: long-term measurement
  • Research topic: ecological informatics and modelling; forest-atmosphere exchange; physiological ecology, population dynamics and species interactions
  • LTER core area: primary production
  • Keywords: air temperature, net radiation, phenology, photosynthetically active radiation, relative humidity, solar radiation
  • Abstract:

    To better quantify the seasonal changes in canopy structure and physiological status (e.g. photosynthetic capacity or efficiency), and provide a more rigorous context for interpretation of the camera data, we installed an extensive set of radiometric instruments on the Harvard Forest Barn Tower beginning in 2011. A complete list of sensors is given in the Methods section. Note that these include both broad-band and narrow-band sensors, and sensors with hemispherical, narrow, and multi-angular fields of view.

  • Methods:

    Instruments

    Instrument: Kipp and Zonen CNR4 (4 channel net radiometer), Delft, the Netherlands. Measures: incoming SW (300-2800 nm), outgoing SW (300-2800 nm), incoming LW (4500-42000 nm), outgoing LW (4500-42000 nm). Location: top of tower (40 m).

    Instrument: Kipp and Zonen PQS1 (quantum sensor), Delft, the Netherlands. Measures: incoming PPFD (400-700 nm), outgoing PPFD (400-700 nm), below-canopy PPFD (400-700 nm). Locations: top of tower (40 m), 1.5 m above ground level at 4 locations.

    Instrument: Delta-T BF-5 (sunshine sensor), Cambridge, UK. Measures: incoming direct PPFD (400-700 nm), incoming diffuse PPFD (400-700 nm). Location: top of tower (30 m).

    Instrument: Skye 1850 (4 channel radiometric sensor), Llandrindod Wells, UK. Measures: incoming and reflected (blue: 470 ± 20 nm, green: 557 ± 25 nm, red: 605 ± 35 nm, NIR: 750 ± 42 nm). Location: top of tower (40 m).

    Instrument: Skye 1850 (4 channel radiometric sensor), Llandrindod Wells, UK. Measures: incoming and reflected (green 1: 530 ± 11 nm, green 2: 570 ± 10 nm, red: 650 ± 55 nm, NIR: 860 ± 60 nm). Location: top of tower (40 m).

    Instrument: Ryu LED (2 channel sensor), custom built: see Ryu et al. (2010). Measures: incoming and reflected, red and NIR bands (red: 646±56 nm, NIR: 843±72 nm). Location: top of tower (40 m).

  • Use:

    This dataset is released to the public and may be freely downloaded. Please keep the designated Contact person informed of any plans to use the dataset. Consultation or collaboration with the original investigators is strongly encouraged. Publications and data products that make use of the dataset must include proper acknowledgement. For more information on LTER Network data access and use policies, please see: http://www.lternet.edu/data/netpolicy.html.

  • Citation:

    Richardson A, Aubrecht D. 2015. Radiometric and Meteorological Data from Harvard Forest Barn Tower Since 2011. Harvard Forest Data Archive: HF249.
